# Before / After

## Landing

Before: The landing page explained the product well but relied mostly on text and a mock itinerary card.

After: The first viewport now combines strong travel typography, a real destination image, a masked hero treatment, and staggered entry motion. It better communicates an AI travel product immediately.

## Auth, Forgot Password, Reset Password

Before: Auth pages were clean and functional.

After: They inherit the stronger global typography, button motion, card glass treatment, and toast styling. Password reset remains consistent with the auth experience.

## Dashboard

Before: Dashboard stats and itinerary cards were usable but static.

After: Cards have richer depth, hover elevation, animated route navigation, and metric count-up effects for a more polished workspace feel.

## Upload

Before: Drag-and-drop upload was clear and functional.

After: The upgraded card, button, focus, and skeleton styling make the upload flow feel more premium without changing behavior.

## Itinerary Detail / Public Share

Before: Itinerary details already showed extracted data, timeline, recommendations, checklist, edit, share, and PDF export.

After: It inherits the new design system polish. Public share responses now avoid exposing internal owner/file metadata.

## Profile

Before: Profile had editable name, read-only email, avatar initials, and stats.

After: Profile stats now animate into view and the overall card/CTA treatment is more refined.
